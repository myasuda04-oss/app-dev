#  *American Psycho* (2000)

> *“I have to return some videotapes.”*  
> — **Patrick Bateman**

---

##  Overview
**American Psycho** is a *psychological thriller* and *satirical horror film* directed by **Mary Harron**, based on the 1991 novel by **Bret Easton Ellis**.  
It explores the double life of **Patrick Bateman**, a wealthy New York investment banker who hides his violent psychopathic ego beneath a perfect social façade.

---

##  Main Characters
- **Patrick Bateman** — Investment banker by day, serial killer by night.  
- **Evelyn Williams** — Bateman’s fiancée.  
- **Paul Allen** — Colleague and one of Bateman’s unfortunate victims.  
- **Detective Donald Kimball** — The investigator who suspects Bateman.  

---

##  Themes and Symbolism
| Theme | Description | Example Scene |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **Consumerism** | Obsession with status, wealth, and image | Business card comparison scene |
| **Identity & Insanity** | Blurred lines between reality and delusion | Final confession |
| **Satire of the 1980s** | Critique of materialism and moral decay | Nightclub and office scenes |

##  Favorite Quote
> “There is an idea of a Patrick Bateman, some kind of abstraction. But there is no real me: only an entity, something illusory.”

---

##  Why I Like It
1. **Clever satire** on corporate culture and vanity.  
2. **Christian Bale’s performance** is both chilling and hilarious.  
3. The **ambiguous ending** keeps you questioning what was real.  
4. Iconic **visual style** and **dark humor**.  

---

##  Fun Fact
- The movie’s **business card scene** is often used in memes and parodies for its absurd seriousness about paper texture and font choice.
